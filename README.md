 Shopify Revenue Optimization Analysis

 Overview
This project focuses on analyzing e-commerce transaction data to identify revenue leaks, growth opportunities, and operational inefficiencies.

Rather than just visualizing data, the goal of this analysis is to answer a critical question:

“What is limiting revenue growth, and what specific actions can improve it?”


  Business Problems Addressed

Through this analysis, I identified and investigated the following key business challenges:

- Over-reliance on repeat customers for revenue
- Revenue concentration in a small number of products
- Underperforming products reducing overall efficiency
- Geographic revenue imbalance
- Unclear long-term sustainability of revenue growth

  Key Findings & Solutions

 1. Heavy Dependence on Repeat Customers

Problem:  
A significant portion of total revenue comes from repeat customers, indicating that the business is highly dependent on existing users rather than consistently acquiring new ones.

Risk: 
If customer retention drops, revenue will decline rapidly.

Solution:  
- Implement a customer acquisition strategy (paid ads, partnerships, referrals)  
- Introduce a first-time buyer incentive (discounts, free shipping)  
- Track Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) vs Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

 2. Revenue Concentration in Few Products

Problem: 
A small number of products generate a large percentage of total revenue.

Risk: 
This creates a single point of failure if demand drops for these products, total revenue is heavily impacted.

Solution:
- Identify mid-performing products and promote them through bundling  
- Run A/B pricing tests to optimize margins  
- Improve visibility of other products via homepage placement and recommendations  


 3. Consistently Underperforming Products

Problem:
Several products contribute little to no revenue.

Impact:  
- Ties up inventory  
- Increases storage and operational costs  
- Reduces overall efficiency  

Solution:  
- Apply discounting or clearance strategies  
- Remove low-performing products from inventory  
- Investigate root causes:
  - Poor pricing  
  - Low visibility  
  - Weak demand  


 4. Geographic Revenue Imbalance

Problem:
Revenue is heavily concentrated in the United Kingdom.

Risk: 
- High exposure to a single market  
- Limited growth potential if the market saturates  

Solution: 
- Expand into high-potential regions (e.g., Europe, North America)  
- Localize marketing campaigns (currency, language, shipping options)  
- Use targeted ads in underperforming regions  


 5. Revenue Growth Without Diversification

Problem:  
Revenue is increasing over time, but growth is driven by the same customers and products.

Risk: 
Growth may not be sustainable long-term.

Solution:  
- Diversify product offerings  
- Invest in new customer segments
- Monitor growth using:
  - Customer segmentation analysis  
  - Cohort analysis
